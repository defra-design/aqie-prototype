Nitrogen dioxide is a colourless gas with an irritating pungent odour. It readily dissolves in water and is one of the main chemicals that causes acid rain.

Long term exposure

The Air Quality Standards Regulations 2010 require the annual average concentration of nitrogen dioxide to not exceed 40 micrograms/m3.
