Particulate matter (PM), known as PM2.5 and PM10, are tiny pieces of solids or liquids in the air. They come from sources such as car tyres, brake and exhaust dust, wood burning and pollen.

These particules can enter your lungs and bloodstream and contribute to:

  • heart disease
  • strokes
  • respiratory problems
